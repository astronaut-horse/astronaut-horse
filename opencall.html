Open Call to Artists

As a visual artist I don't yet know how I feel about AI, especially tools that threaten any aspect of how artists survive.

In late 2022 I was given the opportunity to start learning about practical AI and machine learning tool development with the guidance of an inspiring group of software engineers and researchers at the Recurse Center.

With their help I've been working on a series of collaborations with other visual artists, building a handful of free software tools that train an open-source AI model (Stable Diffusion) on sets of artist-provided images and artworks which you can explore on this site.

Last month I was given a hardware grant from an AI research and development group that allows me to expand this project and invite more artists to collaborate.

The project is focused on...

  • ● developing free AI tools for artists by artists
  • ● co-working over automation
  • ● building critical agency
  • ● avoiding AI hype

Key points for artists...

  • ● collaborating artists aren't obligated to do anything
  • ● you can remain anonymous or be credited
  • ● this is not a for-profit project
  • ● artist data and privacy is fully respected
  • ● freely train AI models on your work or images
  • ● freely prompt custom-trained models to make images
  • ● use AI images you generate in any way you want
  • ● optionally participate in upcoming exhibitions
  • ● optionally learn to build your own AI tools
  • ● optionally share your thoughts

If you're curious about collaborating check out the links below or reach out to me directly. I'd be excited to hear your thoughts on the project and potentially work together.
